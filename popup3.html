The Clinic Session

So, I booked an MES session at the public clinic in Kreuzberg. It felt strange, almost too casual. Like signing up for any simple procedure. I chose the standard edit, no extras. It was cheap and quick. Outside, a glowing blue sign read “No Pain, Just Peace.”

The clinic itself was bright and clean. The receptionist didn't say much—just handed me the intake tablet and went back to her screen. I typed in the memory to erase: “Warsaw frontline, November 2055.” The simple words made the event feel small and distant, like it wasn't even mine.

As I waited, I noticed the clinic's advertisements looping on a screen. They showed smiling faces, soldiers reunited with families, peaceful and free from past traumas. The message was simple: “Leave the past behind.” But watching that screen, I felt uneasy. Could forgetting be that simple? Could pain be erased without losing something important?

A nurse came out and called my name. I stood up and followed her down a narrow hallway into a small room. It was colder than I expected, and the air had that stale, plastic kind of smell. She told me to take a seat. Then, without much small talk, she started attaching the electrodes to my temples...

As she started counting down, something in me hesitated. Not fear exactly, but a kind of resistance. I thought of all the things I had buried deep—faces, places, sounds. Part of me wondered what would be left if those were gone. Then, everything went black.

When I came to, everything around me looked the same. The nurse was standing nearby. She gave me a faint smile and asked how I felt. I didn't know what to say, so I just nodded.
